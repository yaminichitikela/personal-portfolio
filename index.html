About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Old Dominion University, specializing in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning with applications in behavioral health. My research focuses on leveraging mobile sensor data and digital phenotyping to develop predictive models that support early clinical intervention.

With a strong background in full-stack web development and data-driven systems, I aim to bridge the gap between cutting-edge AI research and real-world healthcare applications that improve patient outcomes.

Research Interests

AI/ML for Behavioral Health

Developing predictive models using mobile sensor data and mental health surveys to identify early signs of patient disengagement, enabling timely and personalized clinical intervention.

Digital Phenotyping

Leveraging passively collected smartphone data (GPS, accelerometer, screen usage) to create digital biomarkers that reflect behavioral and mental health states.

Natural Language Processing

Applying NLP techniques to build intelligent conversational systems, including recommendation engines that understand user intent and context.
